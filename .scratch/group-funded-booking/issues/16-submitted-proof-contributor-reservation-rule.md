## Question

Should a contributor's submitted payment proof temporarily reserve a required contributor position before vendor verification, and when does that reservation end?

## Resolution

Yes. A submitted payment proof creates a temporary contributor reservation immediately. It occupies one of the campaign's required contributor positions, preventing over-submission while the vendor reviews proof. It does not increase verified funding, funded amount, or the count used to advance the campaign to vendor review.

The reservation ends when the vendor rejects the proof, releasing the position for another contributor. There is no separate automatic reservation timeout: a still-pending proof remains reserved until the campaign funding deadline, at which point it remains unverified and does not count toward funding under the campaign's normal deadline outcome.

UI meaning: verified positions are locked and green; submitted-proof reservations are pending vendor verification and blue; unclaimed positions are vacant and gray.
